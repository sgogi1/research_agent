RESEARCH REPORT: HORNSBY NSW PROPERTY MARKET
Last Updated: 2023

BACKGROUND
• Hornsby is a major suburban center located 19km northwest of Sydney CBD
• Historically developed as a railway junction town in the late 1800s
• Serves as administrative center of Hornsby Shire Council
• Known for bushland setting and position as gateway to Sydney's upper North Shore

CURRENT STATE
Housing Stock & Prices
• Median house price: Approximately $1.6-1.8M (2023)
• Median unit price: Approximately $750,000-850,000
• Mix of housing types: Free-standing homes (60%), apartments (35%), townhouses (5%)

Market Characteristics
• Strong transport links (rail junction, multiple bus routes)
• Major shopping/commercial center
• Well-regarded schools and healthcare facilities
• High proportion of owner-occupiers
• Growing appeal to young families priced out of lower North Shore

KEY DEBATES
Development vs. Conservation
• Tension between increased density and environmental preservation
• Council planning controls balancing growth with heritage protection
• Debate over appropriate height limits for new developments

Infrastructure Capacity
• Questions about infrastructure keeping pace with population growth
• Parking pressures in town center
• School capacity concerns

RISKS & LIMITATIONS
Market Vulnerabilities
• Interest rate sensitivity due to high mortgage levels
• Dependent on Sydney's broader economic performance
• Infrastructure bottlenecks during peak hours

Geographic Constraints
• Bushfire risk in some areas
• Limited expansion potential due to national parks
• Topographical challenges for new development

POTENTIAL FUTURE DIRECTIONS
• Continued apartment development near transport hub
• Focus on "missing middle" housing types
• Potential for commercial office expansion
• Enhanced retail/hospitality precincts
• Implementation of Hornsby Town Center masterplan
