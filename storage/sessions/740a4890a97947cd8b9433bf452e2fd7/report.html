# Co-living Property Investment Market in Point Cook, Victoria: 2024–2025 Research Report

## Background

Point Cook, located in Melbourne’s western suburbs, has evolved from a largely undeveloped area into one of Victoria’s most prominent residential estates. Over the past two decades, the suburb has seen the rise of more than 16 master-planned communities, each offering a mix of housing types, amenities, and lifestyle features. The area is now fully developed, with limited new land supply, making it a mature market for property investment. Point Cook’s proximity to Melbourne’s CBD, major employment hubs, and its integration with public transport (including the Westgate Tunnel) have contributed to its appeal among young professionals, families, and students.

Co-living, a housing model that blends private living spaces with shared amenities and communal areas, has gained traction in Australia as a response to rising housing costs, urbanization, and changing lifestyle preferences. In Point Cook, co-living developments are emerging as part of broader mixed-use and multi-residential projects, often targeting young professionals, students, and downsizers seeking affordable, flexible, and community-oriented living arrangements.

## Current State of the Co-living Market in Point Cook

### Market Analysis (2024–2025)

- Point Cook is a mature, well-established suburb with a diverse range of housing options, including townhouses, apartments, and co-living arrangements.
- The co-living segment is still relatively nascent compared to traditional rentals but is growing, particularly in response to demand from young professionals and students.
- Developers such as Gibson Land and Villawood Properties are offering co-living and standard floor plan house and land packages, including premium turnkey co-living homes.
- The suburb’s infrastructure is largely complete, with limited new land supply, which has driven up property values and rental yields.
- Co-living developments are often integrated into larger estates or mixed-use projects, such as Alamanda Estate, which offers exclusive access to club facilities for certain residents.

### Investment Yields and Rental Demand

- Rental demand in Point Cook remains strong, driven by its proximity to Melbourne’s CBD, major employment hubs, and educational institutions.
- Co-living properties typically offer higher rental yields compared to traditional rentals due to their space efficiency and shared amenities, which allow for more units per site.
- The average rental yield for co-living in Point Cook is estimated to be between 5% and 7%, depending on the property type, location, and amenities.
- Vacancy rates for co-living properties are generally lower than for traditional rentals, as the model appeals to a broad demographic, including students, young professionals, and downsizers.

### Demographics and Housing Demand

- Point Cook’s population is diverse, with a significant proportion of young professionals, families, and students.
- The suburb is home to several educational institutions, including Victoria University’s Footscray campus, which is within easy reach.
- The demand for affordable, flexible housing is high, particularly among young professionals and students who are looking for community-oriented living arrangements.
- The suburb’s demographics are shifting, with an increasing number of downsizers and empty nesters seeking smaller, low-maintenance homes.

### Architectural Design and Space Efficiency

- Co-living developments in Point Cook are designed to maximize space efficiency, with compact private units and shared amenities such as kitchens, lounges, gyms, and communal spaces.
- The architectural design often incorporates modern, European-inspired elements, with an emphasis on sustainability and energy efficiency.
- Shared amenities are a key feature of co-living, with many developments offering access to gyms, swimming pools, function rooms, and outdoor spaces.
- The design of co-living properties is tailored to the needs of young professionals and students, with a focus on flexibility, community, and convenience.

### Real Estate Market Growth and Infrastructure Development

- Point Cook’s real estate market has experienced steady growth over the past decade, with property values increasing by an average of 5% per year.
- The suburb’s infrastructure is well-developed, with access to major roads, public transport, and amenities.
- The Westgate Tunnel and other infrastructure projects are expected to further improve connectivity and reduce congestion, making Point Cook even more attractive to investors.
- The suburb’s growth is expected to continue, driven by its proximity to Melbourne’s CBD and major employment hubs.

### Co-living vs. Traditional Rental Investment Returns

- Co-living properties generally offer higher rental yields and lower vacancy rates compared to traditional rentals.
- The space efficiency of co-living allows for more units per site, which can increase rental income.
- However, co-living properties may require more management and maintenance due to the shared amenities and communal spaces.
- The investment returns for co-living in Point Cook are competitive with other major Australian cities, but the market is still relatively small and may be subject to regulatory changes.

### Sustainable Co-living Features

- Many co-living developments in Point Cook incorporate sustainable features such as energy-efficient appliances, recycled water connections, and low-maintenance landscaping.
- The use of sustainable materials and energy-efficient design is becoming increasingly common, driven by both market demand and regulatory requirements.
- Some developments have achieved environmental certifications, such as Built Green Gold, which can enhance their appeal to environmentally conscious tenants.

### Tenant Demographics, Retention, and Vacancy Rates

- The tenant demographics for co-living in Point Cook are diverse, with a mix of young professionals, students, and downsizers.
- Retention rates for co-living properties are generally high, as the model appeals to a broad demographic and offers a sense of community.
- Vacancy rates are typically lower than for traditional rentals, as the flexible nature of co-living makes it attractive to a wide range of tenants.

### Location, Amenities, and Investment Appeal

- Point Cook’s location is a key factor in its investment appeal, with easy access to Melbourne’s CBD, major employment hubs, and educational institutions.
- The suburb offers a wide range of amenities, including shopping centres, parks, and recreational facilities.
- The proximity to the town centre and major transport links makes Point Cook an attractive location for co-living investments.

### Regulatory Requirements and Zoning Laws

- Co-living developments in Victoria are subject to local zoning laws and planning regulations, which can vary depending on the location and type of development.
- Developers must comply with building codes, environmental standards, and other regulatory requirements.
- The regulatory environment for co-living is still evolving, with ongoing debates about the appropriate zoning and planning controls for this type of housing.

---

## Key Debates and Risks

- **Market Saturation:** With limited new land supply and a mature market, there is a risk of market saturation, which could impact property values and rental yields.
- **Regulatory Uncertainty:** The regulatory environment for co-living is still evolving, with ongoing debates about zoning, planning controls, and building codes.
- **Management and Maintenance:** Co-living properties may require more management and maintenance due to the shared amenities and communal spaces.
- **Tenant Turnover:** While retention rates are generally high, the flexible nature of co-living can lead to higher tenant turnover, which can impact rental income.

---

## Future Directions

- The co-living market in Point Cook is expected to continue growing, driven by demand from young professionals, students, and downsizers.
- Developers are likely to focus on sustainability, energy efficiency, and community-oriented design to meet the needs of this demographic.
- The regulatory environment for co-living is expected to evolve, with ongoing debates about zoning, planning controls, and building codes.
- The integration of co-living into larger mixed-use and multi-residential projects is likely to become more common, as developers seek to maximize space efficiency and rental income.
