RESEARCH REPORT: HORNSBY NSW PROPERTY MARKET ANALYSIS

1. BACKGROUND
• Hornsby is a major suburban center located 19km northwest of Sydney CBD
• Historically developed as a railway junction town in the late 1800s
• Serves as an administrative center for the Hornsby Shire Council
• Traditional market characterized by family homes and increasing medium-density development

2. CURRENT STATE
Housing Stock:
• Mix of standalone houses (approximately 60%) and apartments (40%)
• Median house price: ~$1.6M (2023)
• Median unit price: ~$750,000 (2023)

Key Market Characteristics:
• Strong transport links (rail junction, multiple bus routes)
• Major shopping/commercial precinct
• Well-regarded schools and healthcare facilities
• Green spaces including Hornsby Quarry development
• Growing professional demographic

3. KEY MARKET DEBATES
Development Intensity:
• Ongoing discussion about appropriate density levels
• Tension between heritage preservation and modernization
• Impact of NSW government housing targets

Infrastructure Capacity:
• Transport congestion concerns
• Adequacy of social infrastructure for growing population
• Parking availability in commercial center

4. RISKS & LIMITATIONS
Market Risks:
• Interest rate sensitivity
• Potential oversupply of apartments
• Infrastructure capacity constraints

Geographic Limitations:
• Topographical constraints limiting development
• Bushfire risk zones
• Limited greenfield development opportunities

5. FUTURE DIRECTIONS
Short-term (1-3 years):
• Continued apartment development near transport hub
• Infrastructure upgrades including Hornsby Quarry parklands
• Retail precinct revitalization

Long-term (5+ years):
• Increased density around transport nodes
• Aging population driving downsizer demand
• Evolution as a major regional center
