### Investment Potential of House Flipping in Hornsby, NSW

---

#### Background and Recent Market Trends

Over the past 5 to 10 years, **Hornsby’s median house prices have shown steady growth**, with a reported increase of approximately **46.39% to 50.85% over the last 5 years** according to different sources, indicating a strong upward trajectory in property values[2][3]. Median house prices currently hover around **$1.8 million to $1.85 million**, with units averaging around **$700,000**[2][4][7].

In the last 12 months, house price growth has been relatively modest or flat, with some reports indicating a slight increase of around **0.6% to 0.7%**, while others note a slight decline of about **-0.28% to -1.67%** for houses, suggesting some recent market stabilization or minor correction[2][4]. Sales volume remains robust with around **100+ houses and several hundred units sold annually**, and recent quarterly sales show active market participation[2][4].

---

#### Demand-to-Supply Ratio and Market Comparison

Hornsby’s property demand appears strong relative to supply, as evidenced by **median days on market for houses at around 30-34 days**, which is faster than units (around 29-49 days), indicating higher demand for houses and quicker turnover[2][4]. Compared to nearby affluent suburbs like Wahroonga, Hornsby’s median house prices are about **36.4% lower**, making it a more affordable option with potential for value uplift[2]. This price gap combined with steady demand suggests favorable conditions for house flipping, as investors can buy below premium prices and capitalize on market appreciation or renovation gains.

---

#### Time on Market and Resale Feasibility

The **average time houses spend on the market in Hornsby is approximately 30 to 34 days**, which is relatively short and conducive to quick resale strategies typical in house flipping[2][4]. Units take slightly longer, but houses’ faster sales cycles improve the feasibility of flipping by reducing holding costs and exposure to market volatility.

---

#### Rental Yields and Investor Returns

Rental yields in Hornsby are moderate, with **house rental yields around 2.45% and unit yields approximately 4.24%**[4]. Rental prices for houses have increased by about **7-8% over the past year**, reflecting strong rental demand and supporting investor returns through rental income during holding periods[2][4]. This rental growth also contributes to property value appreciation, enhancing the attractiveness of flipping investments.

---

#### Demographic and Economic Drivers

Hornsby’s population growth has been modest, around **1.3% from 2016 to 2021**, with a population of about 22,462 people[3]. The demographic profile shows a predominant age group of **30-39 years**, with many households consisting of couples with children, often in professional occupations[3]. This stable, family-oriented demographic supports ongoing demand for quality housing.

Local employment trends align with professional and service sectors, which underpin steady housing demand. The presence of parks and amenities covering a significant portion of the suburb (40.4%) adds to the suburb’s appeal[3].

---

#### Market Stability and Growth Compared to Greater Sydney

Hornsby’s property market has shown **slightly stronger median unit price growth (2.4% p.a.) compared to Greater Sydney (2.1% p.a.) over the past 5 years**, indicating relative resilience[1]. However, house price growth has been more modest recently, reflecting broader Sydney market trends with some cooling after rapid growth phases[2][4][6].

Compared to Greater Sydney, Hornsby offers a more affordable entry point with stable demand, making it attractive for investors seeking moderate growth and lower volatility.

---

#### Renovation Costs and Regulatory Considerations

Typical renovation costs for house flipping in Hornsby would align with Greater Sydney averages, where moderate to extensive renovations can range from **$50,000 to over $150,000** depending on property size and scope. Regulatory considerations include compliance with **local council zoning laws, heritage overlays, and building permits** managed by Hornsby Shire Council[1]. Renovators must navigate these regulations carefully to avoid delays and additional costs.

---

#### Risks and Challenges

Key risks in flipping houses in Hornsby include:

- **Market volatility:** Recent slight declines or flat growth suggest potential short-term price stagnation or correction risks[4].
- **Holding costs:** Given median days on market of about a month, extended holding periods due to market shifts or renovation delays can increase costs.
- **Regulatory hurdles:** Planning approvals and compliance can slow project timelines.
- **Economic factors:** Broader economic conditions affecting borrowing costs and buyer demand could impact resale prices.

---

#### Local Government Policies and Development Impact

Hornsby Shire Council’s policies emphasize sustainable development and maintaining the suburb’s character, which can limit high-density redevelopment but support quality residential upgrades[1]. Zoning laws generally favor low to medium density residential use, which suits house flipping but may restrict large-scale redevelopment opportunities. Future development plans focus on infrastructure improvements that could enhance property values.
